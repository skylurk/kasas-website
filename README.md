1. Hero — full screen, immersive
A cinematic full-bleed video or image of the savannah/bush airstrip. Large editorial headline, short subtitle, CTA. Gradient from bottom. Transparent navbar already handles itself.

2. Intro Statement — dark bg, large type
A single bold paragraph that sells the feeling, not the service. Something like "Most people fly to their safari. Our clients fly into it." Big typography, minimal layout. Sets the tone immediately.

3. Destinations Grid — the meat of the page
Cards for the key safari destinations Kasas flies to — Masai Mara, Amboseli, Samburu, Lewa, Tsavo etc. Each card has a background image, destination name, a short note, and flight time from Nairobi. Same ServiceCard pattern we built but adapted for destinations. 2 columns desktop, 1 mobile.

4. The Experience — alternating split sections
Two or three split sections covering what makes the charter experience different — the aircraft, the flexibility, the bush strip access. Text one side, photo the other. Warm tones, not black/white clinical. Think rich dark greens, amber, earth tones.

5. Why Charter vs Scheduled — comparison or feature list
A clean section that addresses the obvious question — why not just fly commercial? Could be a simple two-column comparison or a list of advantages. White background, simple and readable.

6. Testimonials — dark bg
Two or three client quotes. Simple, elegant. No star ratings — that feels too e-commerce. Just the quote, name, and a subtle detail like "Nairobi to Mara, March 2024".

7. CTA Banner — full width
Book your safari charter. Warm, inviting. Different energy from the Dornier CTA — less urgent, more aspirational.
